📖 Scientific Reference & Score Validation Guide

We use validated clinical and behavioral metrics to determine your digital wellness state. Here is how we define each component and the research supporting it:

🧠 Current Mood (PHQ-9 Baseline)

Based on standard self-reported emotional valence tests. Tracked to monitor acute affective states. Persistent low mood indicates high support needs.

📈 Stress Level (PSS-10 Scale)

Adapted from the Perceived Stress Scale (PSS-10). It gauges how unpredictable, uncontrollable, and overloaded you feel your life is. A score of 7+ indicates high acute stress requiring micro-intervention exercises.

😴 Sleep Quality (PSQI Criteria)

Informed by the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). Measures subjective sleep latency, duration, and restorative efficiency over a 10-point self-assessment scale.

📱 Screen Time & Digital Wellness (WHO Guidelines)

Calculates active study hours and digital eye strain risks. Grounded in research suggesting that screen time above 10 hours daily correlates with cognitive fatigue and reduced attention spans.

👥 Social Interaction (LSNS-6 Index)

Derived from the Lubben Social Network Scale (LSNS-6). Self-rated peer/family connection levels. Active social communication (5+/10) acts as a powerful buffer against study burnout.

🧘 Break Frequency (Pomodoro Fatigue Management)

Tracks periodic rest intervals during long study sessions. Aligns with Pomodoro/active rest guidelines advocating for a 5-10 minute pause every 25-50 minutes of focused academic work.
